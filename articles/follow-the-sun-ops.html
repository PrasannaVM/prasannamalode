What managing a 16-person global team taught me about follow-the-sun ops

January 2026 · 7 min read · Prasanna Malode

Running a follow-the-sun operation sounds simple on paper: coverage across timezones, seamless handovers, 24×7 continuity. The reality is messier — and more human — than any playbook suggests.

The Handover Problem

The single biggest failure mode in follow-the-sun is the handover. Information gets lost. Context doesn't transfer. The incoming shift restarts investigations the outgoing shift already half-solved. We fixed this with a structured handover template — not long, not bureaucratic, but specific about what's in-flight, what's blocked, and what the next 2 hours need.

A 5-minute handover done well saves 2 hours of re-investigation. Most teams skip it because they're busy. That busyness is exactly why they need it.

Performance Monitoring Across Shifts

You can't manage what you can't see across 8 timezones. We built real-time dashboards giving visibility into SLA compliance, incident queue depth, and team velocity — regardless of shift. This wasn't about surveillance; it was about being able to unblock people quickly and spot patterns that no single shift could see alone.

The One Ritual That Keeps Teams Aligned

Weekly all-hands across shifts — 20 minutes, same agenda every time: metrics review, one learning from the week, one shoutout. Simple. Consistent. It built the shared identity that makes distributed teams function like a team rather than isolated shifts doing disconnected work.

Mentoring Across Distance

Leading two managers and two DevOps Architects remotely forced me to be more intentional about development than I ever was in person. Written feedback became more important. Regular 1:1s became non-negotiable. Career conversations happened on a cadence — not when someone was already considering leaving.

The single biggest thing I learned: presence is not about timezone overlap. It's about reliability. If your team knows you'll respond, unblock, and follow through — they feel supported regardless of where you are.
